Paid funnel (last 90 days)
ⓘ —
Close date vs click date. We count revenue by when a deal closed; HubSpot Ads counts by when the ad was clicked (ad interaction inside the date range). With your ~29-day-plus sales cycle, recent windows undercount in HubSpot — its ROI climbed from −46% (30d) to +37% (90d) purely as clicks matured. (See the maturity bar above.)
ROAS vs ROI — different formula, same data. We show ROAS = revenue ÷ spend. HubSpot shows ROI = (revenue − spend) ÷ spend. So our 2.93x ROAS is the same as +193% ROI; HubSpot's "37% ROI" equals 1.37x ROAS.
Lifecycle stage (a HubSpot data-hygiene gap). HubSpot's ROI is built to count a deal once its contact reaches the Customer lifecycle stage. Your paid contacts never advance past Sales Qualified Lead (0 are Customer), so HubSpot's native Customer/ROI reporting is unreliable for you. We count every closed-won deal regardless of lifecycle. Worth fixing in HubSpot: a workflow that sets Lifecycle = Customer when a deal is won.
Ad-click coverage (Last-click / All only). We can only credit ad-clickers who left a captured gclid on the contact record (~28% of HubSpot-tracked clicks don't). HubSpot's Ads tool tracks every click server-side, so its Last/All counts read higher. First-interaction matches closely.
Credit model — we match HubSpot. Both credit the full deal amount to each ad-touched deal (single-touch), not a fractional multi-touch split.
Currency. Both in USD (company currency), matching HubSpot's default ROI currency setting.

⚡ Revenue Opportunity Engineⓘ
—
Answers “if we improve one keyword’s ranking this month, which earns the most added revenue?” Ranks organic queries at position 4–15 by a Revenue Opp. estimate = click upside to reach the top 3 × your organic revenue-per-click (from HubSpot closed-won) × how well the query converts (its Google Ads conversion rate) × how reachable the rank gain is. 🎯 Commercial Booth mode (default) re-ranks by booth-buying intent — the Intent column (0–100) scores each query on product/category terms (booth, soundproof, vocal, audiology, office…), buying terms (cost, price, quote, package…), paid presence, and product-page match — so booth-selling queries outrank high-traffic blog terms. All Organic keeps every opportunity for content/traffic research. Ads CVR is that same query’s paid conversion rate when we run it (— = no paid data, score falls back to the channel baseline). Per-query organic leads / closed revenue can’t be shown directly — Google withholds the organic search term — so the engine bridges it with the paid signal plus channel-level organic $/click. Estimates, for prioritization.
